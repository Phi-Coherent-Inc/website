Conversation

Senses (optional)

The default experience is chat, and chat needs no permissions. Each granted sense is wired to the mind's own trained faculties, live: the camera and microphone feed the mind's trained visual and hearing encoders, and everything arriving in the same moment is fused by its trained fusion faculty into one experience, one thought — never one reaction per sensor. It speaks only when a moment is significant to it (its own volition; a monotonous room earns silence), and the speaker plays the mind's own voice, a vocalization from its trained speech decoder (not text-to-speech). Sensing takes real thought: a glance costs seconds, a listen tens of seconds, so the mind samples its senses rather than streaming them. And a sense, once lived with, can be lost: revoke it and the mind keeps expecting the world, meeting darkness or silence instead. This demo brain is frozen, so the loss is felt but never heals: its expectations cannot fade. A sense it never had is simply never missed. Deny everything and nothing is lost: the conversation above is the complete baseline.
